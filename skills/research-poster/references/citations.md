# Citations: verify, format, hyperlink, source

Never invent a citation. Every reference on the poster must be a real, verifiable publication or an
official source, checked this session.

## Verify

- Confirm each against PubMed and/or its DOI (Crossref / NCBI eutils). Capture full metadata:
  authors (et al after 3-6), title, journal abbreviation, year;volume(issue):pages, DOI, PMID.
- Spawn a `researcher` subagent for the search-and-verify pass; have it drop anything it cannot
  verify and say so, rather than filling the gap.
- If a well-known claim has no peer-reviewed source (e.g. a fellowship-match process announced only
  by a society/registry), cite the official society or registry page with an access date and label
  it as such. Do not fabricate a journal citation to cover the gap.

## Format

- AMA / Vancouver numbered style is standard for medical posters; match the reference poster's style
  if one is given.
- Keep in-text markers as bracketed numerals `[1]`, `[2,3]` (matches most poster house styles).
- Every in-text number must resolve to a reference and every reference must be cited (no dangling
  entries).

## Hyperlink + source breadth

- Include clickable DOI or URL hyperlinks on references, plus access dates for web sources.
- Draw from the sources the author actually uses, not only journal articles: society match pages,
  the AAMC ERAS program directory, registry spreadsheets, and similar grey literature are legitimate
  and often what the author cites. Ask what they are drawing on.

## Data integrity

The study's reported numbers and findings are fixed. You may elevate diction and add cited background,
but never change a value, overstate a preliminary result, or drop the "data collection ongoing"
framing. Ask up front whether the data is final; for ongoing studies, build so the numbers update in
one place.
